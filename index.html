Phi

Sigma's design system

The majority of my time at Sigma was spent designing, maintaining and educating about our design system, which we dubbed Phi.

Phi has all of the standard design system qualities: cohesion, flexibility, scalability, accessibility, etc. Uniquely, Phi was built to be fully user-themeable, as many of Sigma's customers embedded our product within their own product environments.

UployBot

Giving our internal tools a much needed facelift

Poor little Uploybot. Originally just a tool to help release code to production, over the years it slowly gained more and more features, without ever getting even a once-over from a designer.

My engineering team finally got fed up with the state of Uploybot and tapped me to help design a top-to-bottom rebuild of the tool using Phi. To say the redesign was a success would be an understatement. Engineers company-wide loved the visual upgrade and the streamlined flows. The update also served as a good test example of how easy it was to migrate code to Phi.

Project: Perspective

What if…we could make Workbook theming painless?

When our new VP of Design started, he kicked off a skunkworks project to redesign the look and feel of our Workbooks UI, codenamed Horizon. He then tasked me, as the resident color expert, to take on imaging a new interface for Workbooks theming, codenamed Perspective (get it?).

The goal of the project was to streamline the theming process and lessen the analysis paralysis our less design-savvy users felt when presented with our broad suite of theming options. You can play with the prototype I built below and see if you agree.
