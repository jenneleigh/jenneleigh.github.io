    Figma Thumbnails

    A quick refresh of the design team's Figma thumbnail design

    Background

    Before I joined Figma, one of our designers has put together a template file with a default thumbnail. It worked fine, but had a lot of friction. So much so that the other designers rarely used it.

    One day, I got tired of not being able to easily find the file I was looking for and decided I was going take a little time and work up a new thumbnail.

    Analysis

    • The current thumbnail used color to help indicate file status, but rarely did anyone remember to update it as the project progressed
    • Layout often got cut off in Figma's UI
    • The design was too monotonous, making it hard to pick out files at a glance

    Research

    I spoke with some designers acquaintances at other companies and had them share what they liked and disliked about their org's thumbnails

    I identified some common patterns among those who liked their thumbnails, most notably being the bold visuals.

    From their, I pinpointed some file data that would be useful to have at a glance

    • A file name and a subtitle
    • Who owned the file
    • What pod the file applied to

    I briefly considered including file status but decided that, knowing it would rarely get updated, it was better to save space for the other details.

    I also decided to use Unsplash for the visual element. With a large free library, it would be easy to find a fun distinguishing image without having to use up our limited design resources.

    The Design

    I tried a few experiments, eventually landed on the basic design you see above.

    I took design to our weekly crit session and got a positive response from team. The ability to use the photo as a visual pun was a big hit!

    For file owner pill, I had every member of my team pick their favorite color, to add just a little extra personality.

    The Component

    I took that feedback and made a few design changes and then turned the whole thing into a single Figma component, with lots of props to make it an absolute breeze to use.

    Conclusions

    The new thumbnails met my primary goal immediately. I spend was less time hunting around Figma, trying to find the file I'm looking for Plus, I get to make dumbs puns, which is always a bonus!

    A pretty solid outcome for what ended up being less than a half-days works.
